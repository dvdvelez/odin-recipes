Quinoa n Pinto Bean Burger

PATTIES
- 1/2 cup red quinoa, rinsed in fine-mesh strainer and drained
- 1 cup water
- 1 cup finely chopped red onion
- 3/4 cup finely chopped red bell pepper
- 2 teaspoons minced garlic (store-bought for ease of preparation)
- 1 large egg, beaten
- 1 can (15 ounces) low-sodium pinto beans, rinsed and drained
- 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
- 1/2 cup panko bread crumbs
- 1/4 cup finely chopped fresh cilantro or parsley leaves
- 1 teaspoon Sriracha or favorite hot sauce
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon granulated onion powder (not salt)
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

- Favorite mayonnaise (to your delight)
- Store-bought tzatziki sauce
- 2 slices ripe tomatoes (per pattie or to your liking)
- 2 lettuce leaves (per pattie or to your liking)
- Extra-virgin olive oil (for brushing veggie patties)
- Hamburger buns of choice


INSTRUCTIONS
1. In a saucepan over medium-high heat, bring the quinoa, water, and 1 teaspoon salt to a boil. Cover the saucepan and reduce the heat to low. Simmer until the quinoa is tender and the water is almost absorbed, 18 to 20 minutes. Remove from the heat, keep covered, and let stand for 5 minutes. Drain the quinoa in a fine-mesh strainer to remove any excess water. Let cool completely.

2. In a skillet over medium heat, warm 1 tablespoon oil. Add the onion, bell pepper, and garlic. Cook until tender, about 8 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove from the heat and cool completely.

3. In a food processor, combine the quinoa, the onion mixture, and the remaining patty ingredients, including 1 teaspoon salt. Pulse until well combined.

4. Empty out blender ingredients and form four patties of equal size, each about 1/2 inch thick. Refrigerate the patties for about 1 hour until ready to grill.

5. Prepare the grill for direct cooking over medium heat (350℉ to 450℉).

6. Brush the veggie patties on both sides with the extra-virgin olive oil. Grill the patties over direct medium heat, with the lid closed, until browned, 8 to 10 minutes. Flip only ONCE halfway through cooking time.

7. Optionally, hamburger buns can also be toasted to your liking.

8. Once patties are done, build each hamburger starting with the mayonnaise on the bottom bun, lettuce leaves, tomatoe slices, veggie patty, and tzatziki sauce on the top bun.

9. ENJOY!!!
