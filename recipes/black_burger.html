Black Bean Cheeseburger

PATTIES
- 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, rinsed and drained
- 1/4 cup fresh cilantro or parsley leaves
- 1 shallot, thinly sliced
- 1 teaspoon minced garlic (store-bought for ease of preparation)
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- 1 teaspoon granulated onion powder (not salt)
- 1/2 teaspoon granulated garlic powder (not salt)
- 1/2 cup panko bread crumbs
- 1 large egg, beaten
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper

- Favorite mayonnaise (to your liking)
- 1 1/2 teaspoons minced canned chipotle peppers in sauce
- 2 slices ripe tomatoes (per pattie or to your liking;optional)
- 2 small ripe Hass avocados, cut into thin slices (top veggie burger to your liking)
- 2 lettuce leaves (per pattie or to your liking)
- Extra-virgin olive oil (for brushing veggie patties)
- Hamburger buns of choice


INSTRUCTIONS
1. In a food processor, combine veggie patty ingredients (EXCEPT EGG and PANKO BREAD) and pulse 15 to 20 times or until ingredients form a thick, chunky paste. Empty out blender ingredients.

2. In a small bowl whisk the egg until it is well blended. Add the egg and panko bread crumbs to the bowl with the veggie patty paste and combine thoroughly. Form four patties of equal size, each about 3/4 inch thick. Refrigerate the patties for at least 1 hour.

3. Prepare the grill for direct cooking over medium-high heat (400℉ to 500℉).

4. Brush the veggie patties on both sides with the extra-virgin olive oil. Grill the patties over direct medium-high heat, with the lid closed, until browned, 6 to 8 minutes. Flip only ONCE halfway through cooking time.

5. Optionally, hamburger buns can also be toasted to your liking.

6. Once patties are done, build each hamburger starting with the mayonnaise on the bottom bun, lettuce leaves, tomatoe slices, veggie patty, avocado slices, and mayonnaise on the top bun.

7. ENJOY!!!
