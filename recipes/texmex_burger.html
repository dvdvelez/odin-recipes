Tex-Mex Burger

PATTIES
- 1 1/2 pounds ground chuck (80% lean)
- 1 teaspoon granulated onion powder (not salt)
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon granulated garlic powder (not salt)
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

- 1 slice cheddar cheese (per pattie)
- Favorite mayonnaise (to your delight; optional)
- Plain sour cream
- Extra-virgin olive oil (for toasting sourdough bread)
- 2 slices of sourdough bread (per pattie)
- Store-bought or homemade pico de gallo. Pico de gallo recipe can be found here


INSTRUCTIONS
1. Mix the patty ingredients, and then form four patties of equal size, each about 1 inch thick. With your thumb or the back of a spoon, make a shallow indentation about 1 inch wide in the center of the patties. This is to prevent the patties from forming a dome as they cook. Refrigerate the patties until ready to grill.

2. Prepare the grill for direct cooking over medium-high heat (400℉ to 500℉).

3. Grill the patties over direct medium-high heat, with the lid closed, until cooked to well-done (160℉) for a total of 10 minutes. Flip only ONCE halfway through cooking time.
Note: Doneness will depend on thickness of the patty, heat of grill, distance of the patty from the direct heat of the grill, and other factors. Please adjust cooking times accordingly.
For additional cooking times, doneness, and health warnings click here.

4. During the last 30 seconds to 1 minute of grilling time, place slice of cheese on each patty to melt.

5. Brush one side of each of the sourdough slices with the extra-virgin olive oil. Toast to preference. Flip slices and toast the other non-oiled side. Toast slices during last minute of grilling time of patties.

6. Once patties are done, build each hamburger starting with mayonnaise spread over the oily side of the bottom slice, patty with melted cheese, pico de gallo, and sour cream to preference spread over the oily side of the top slice.

7. ENJOY!!!
